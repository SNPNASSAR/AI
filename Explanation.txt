1. Predicted Daily Sales

Example output:

Predicted Daily Sales: $594.52

Meaning:

The machine learning model estimates that if 60 customers visit the café in a day, the café will make approximately $594.52 in sales.

This is only a prediction, not an exact value. Actual sales may be higher or lower depending on factors such as:

What customers buy
Discounts offered
Weather
Special events
2. Slope (Coefficient)

Example:

Slope (Coefficient): 9.81

The slope tells us how much sales increase for each additional customer.

Here it means:

Every additional customer increases daily sales by approximately $9.81.

For example:

Customers	Expected Increase in Sales
+1	+$9.81
+5	+$49.05
+10	+$98.10

This is why the regression line slopes upward.

3. Intercept

Example:

Intercept: 5.95

The intercept is the predicted sales when the number of customers is 0.

Mathematically,

Sales = (Slope × Customers) + Intercept

So,

Sales = (9.81 × Customers) + 5.95

When customers = 0,

Sales = 5.95

In this café example, the intercept does not have much practical meaning, because a café with zero customers would not realistically earn sales. It simply helps position the regression line mathematically.

4. Regression Line

The red line is called the best-fit line.

It represents the relationship learned by the model.

The equation is approximately:

Sales=9.81×Customers+5.95

Every point on the red line is a predicted sales value.

5. Blue Dots

The blue dots are the actual training data.

For example,

Customers	Actual Sales

These are the observations used to train the model.

6. Green Dot

The green dot is the prediction made by the model.

If you enter:

60

the green dot appears at

(60, 594.52)

It shows where the model believes the next data point should be.

7. Why isn't the prediction exactly $600?

You might think:

60 customers × $10 = $600

But the training data isn't perfectly linear.

For example,

35 customers → $340
50 customers → $480

don't follow a perfect "$10 per customer" pattern.

Linear Regression finds the best overall line that minimizes prediction errors across all data points, rather than fitting every point exactly.

Overall Interpretation

The model learned that:

There is a positive relationship between customers and sales.
As the number of customers increases, daily sales also increase.
On average, each additional customer contributes about $9.81 in sales.
The model can estimate future sales for any reasonable number of customers.
